Organs Involved in Breathing
  • The lungs are the main organs involved in human breathing.
  • Air passes through the nose → trachea → lungs.
  • The trachea connects the nose to both the left and right lungs.
  • The left lung is smaller than the right lung because the heart is on the left side.

📊
Rate of Breathing
Rate of breathing = Number of upward AND downward chest movements in one minute.
It depends on the type of activity being done.

Rate Examples of Activities
🟢 Low Rate Watching TV, sleeping, studying, reading
🟡 Moderate Rate Walking slowly, climbing stairs, walking fast
🔴 High Rate Running, cycling, swimming, jumping jacks
📌 Conclusion: The more vigorous (active) the activity, the higher the breathing rate.
🌫️
Situations That Affect Breathing
Haze = Dry air containing various gases, dust, and floating particles in the atmosphere. It causes low visibility and is unhealthy for humans.

Bad for Breathing:
🔥 Open burning / haze 🚬 Smoking 👥 Crowded places
Good for Breathing:
🌳 Clean environment / parks 🏃 Regular exercise 🚭 Not smoking

🚬 Effects of Smoking on Lungs:
  • Lungs become black because of chemicals in cigarettes
  • Lungs get damaged → can lead to lung cancer
  • Smokers have trouble breathing normally
🔄 Inhalation vs Exhalation

🔵 Inhalation (Breathing IN)

  • Air taken INTO the lungs
  • Path: Nose → Trachea → Lungs
  • Chest moves UPWARDS
  • Lungs expand
  • Air enters the lungs
  • Inhaled air: MORE oxygen
  • Inhaled air: LESS carbon dioxide

🔴 Exhalation (Breathing OUT)

  • Air expelled FROM the lungs
  • Path: Lungs → Trachea → Nose
  • Chest moves DOWNWARDS
  • Lungs contract
  • Air exits the lungs
  • Exhaled air: MORE carbon dioxide
  • Exhaled air: LESS oxygen

Key Facts to Remember
  • Exchange of gases takes place in the lungs.
  • Oxygen enters the lungs; carbon dioxide leaves the lungs.
  • The left lung is smaller than the right — the heart is on the left side.
  • Air can also enter the lungs through the mouth (not just the nose).
  • Breathing is the process of inhaling AND exhaling.
